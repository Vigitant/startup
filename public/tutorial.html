How to play Quintus

Quintus is a math game that asks the player to take five numerals and make a valid equation out of them. The numerals must be kept in the same order as they are presented.

The math operations are:

+Addition
-Subtraction
×Multiplication
/Division
^Exponent
√Root
()Parentheses
=Equality


Expressions follow the order of operations (Parentheses, Exponent, Multiplication/Division, Addition/Subtraction).

Roots use whatever number or parenthetical expression precedes it as their basis. For instance, 3√8 would evaluate to 2, (6/3)√4 would evaluate to 2, and √9 at the beginning of an equation would yield an error.

Expressions must contain = to be valid. More than one = can be used if all expressions on either of side of each = are equal. For instance, 8/2=9-5=4.

Numerals next to each other with without any operations between them count as multiple digit numbers. 007 is the same as 7.

Negating numbers, use of decimals, and any other math operations not listed are not allowed.

There may be more than one solution for any given number. For example, 24601 can be solved as 2+4=6×01 or 246^0=1

A couple of hints:

1 to the root or power of anything will always equal 1. 42√1 = 1^27 = 1.

Anything to the power of 0 is 1. 13^0 = 1.

Thanks for reading. Have fun!
